Green Season Guide — Hakuba

Green Season in Hakuba.

At 700m elevation, Hakuba stays cool from spring through autumn. Alpine hikes, riverside cafés, mountain-view onsen, and seasonal soba in old farmhouses — the green season is packed with quiet, local pleasures.

Green Season in Hakuba

Mountains, lakes, and onsen.
Summer is Hakuba's slowest, softest season.

Unlike the bustling ski crowd of winter, summer Hakuba is quiet — birdsong, mountain streams, and starry nights. Hiking, SUP, lakeside play, onsen-hopping, and locals' favourite cafés and soba spots. Pick your pace from the four themes below.

FAQ

FAQ

Is it cold at night even in August?

Yes — nights can drop to 18–20℃. A long-sleeve shirt or light hoodie helps. For 1,400m+ areas (Usagidaira, Happo Pond), bring a fleece.

Can I walk around in sandals?

Around the village, yes. But Happo Pond and Tsugaike Nature Park hikes require sneakers or trekking shoes — staff at lifts will also remind you.

Can I get around without a car?

Yes. Happo, Iwatake, and Tsugaike are reachable by bus and shuttle. Echoland's cafés and izakaya are walkable, and most things are within bike range of UNPLAN.

Can I rent an e-bike?

Ask at the UNPLAN front desk. More rental shops in the village now stock e-bikes too. Book ahead in peak season.

What do I do if it rains?

Classic combo: café-hopping + onsen + izakaya. Mountain Harbor can be magical with sea-of-clouds on misty days — foggy forecasts are sometimes the best time to go.
